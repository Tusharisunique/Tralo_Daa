System Guide

A brief overview of how the TRALO exchange works.

The Engine

TRALO is built around a custom C++ matching engine. It uses Price-Time Priority, meaning orders are filled based on the best price first, then by the time they arrived.

To keep things fast, the engine uses flat price-arrays. This allows it to process roughly 50,000 matches per second without any lag.

Bot Ecosystem

The market is populated by 40 concurrent bots to simulate real activity:

  • Retail Bots: Simulate normal human traders making small, occasional trades.
  • HFT Algos: Fast bots that move in and out of positions quickly to capture small price gaps.
  • Market Makers: These bots provide liquidity by constantly placing both buy and sell orders.

Safety & Limits

The exchange enforces a ±20% Price Band. If you try to place an order too far away from the current base price, the engine will reject it to protect against "fat-finger" typos.

All internal prices are handled in Paise to avoid floating-point errors, ensuring every trade is accurate to the decimal.

Fairness

We track every order from placement to match. In the Fairness Analysis tab, you can compare your average latency against the bots. Because everyone shares the same processing queue, the results should remain balanced.
