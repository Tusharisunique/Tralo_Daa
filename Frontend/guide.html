Core Mechanics

The Matching Engine

At the heart of TRALO is a custom-built C++ matching engine designed for O(1) complexity. Instead of searching through lists of orders, it uses flat price-arrays where every price point is a direct index. This allows the system to process roughly 50,000 orders per second with sub-microsecond internal latency.

The engine follows the industry standard Price-Time Priority rule:

  • Price Priority: A Buy order at a higher price always matches before a lower-priced one. A Sell order at a lower price matches before a higher-priced one.
  • Time Priority: If multiple orders exist at the same price, the one that arrived first is filled first.
Glossary

Trading Terminology

Best Bid
The highest price a buyer is currently willing to pay for a stock. It represents the "front" of the Buy queue.
Best Ask
The lowest price a seller is currently willing to accept. Also known as the "Offer."
Spread
The gap between the Best Ask and the Best Bid. A tighter spread usually indicates a more liquid and healthy market.
Market Depth
The list of all pending orders at different price levels. It shows how much "supply" and "demand" exists beyond the ticker price.
Limit Order
An order to trade at a specific price or better. It doesn't fill immediately unless a matching price exists in the book.
Fill Rate
The percentage of your total orders that resulted in a successful trade (match).
The Ecosystem

Simulated Traders (Bots)

To create a realistic trading environment, the system spawns 40 concurrent bot threads that simulate different market behaviors:

Retail Traders
Simulate individual humans. They place small, infrequent orders with random delays.
HFT Algos
High-Frequency Trading bots. They spray thousands of orders to capture tiny price movements.
Market Makers
Critical for liquidity. They constantly quote both Buy and Sell prices to keep the spread tight.
Snipers
Stealthy bots that wait for specific bursts of volume to execute large "hidden" trades.
Technical FAQ

Common Questions

Why does the Admin stream start at zero?

The C++ Engine starts as soon as the backend launches. However, the Frontend only connects to a Live Stream. The exchange does not store historical bot trades in a database to preserve performance. When you open the Admin page, you are "tuning in" to the live broadcast—you only see events occurring from that moment onwards.

Price Improvement (Better Fills)

If you place a BUY order at ₹3000 while the Best Ask is ₹2500, the exchange will match you at ₹2500. A Limit Order says: "I want to buy at THIS price OR BETTER." Since ₹2500 is better for you than ₹3000, the system gives you the lower price. This is a standard feature of real-world exchanges.

Circuit Breakers (Price Bands)

To prevent "fat-finger" errors (accidentally typing too many zeros), the exchange enforces a ±20% Price Band. If you try to buy a stock trading at ₹2500 for ₹5000, the order will be rejected. This protects traders from extreme losses due to typos.
